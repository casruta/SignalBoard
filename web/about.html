Project

Finds diamonds in the dirt. A fundamentals-driven engine focused on small–mid cap equities ($300M–$20B) — the under-researched, undervalued, quality companies that institutional coverage overlooks.

Stocks are ranked solely by DCF undervaluation — the margin of safety between intrinsic value and market price. Quality metrics serve as hard safety gates, not scoring dimensions.

Methodology

A quantitative pipeline that screens ~3,000 US equities through Damodaran-aligned safety filters, then ranks survivors by DCF margin of safety.

DCF Model

Damodaran-aligned FCFF (Free Cash Flow to Firm) discounted at WACC, built on 4 years of historical financial data per company. Bottom-up beta, synthetic cost of debt via interest coverage spreads, convex competitive fade, scenario analysis (bear/base/bull), terminal value cross-check (Gordon growth vs exit multiple), and Altman Z-based distress probability adjustment.

Safety Gates

  • Market cap $300M – $20B
  • Altman Z-Score > 3.0
  • ≥ 4 quarters financial data
  • Revenue not declining
  • Piotroski F-Score ≥ 5/9
  • Positive free cash flow
  • ROIC > WACC
  • Adequate liquidity
  • Minimum trading volume
  • DCF margin of safety ≥ 15%
