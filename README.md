## Governing Party & Premier Timeline

| Period | Premier | Party | Key Context |
|:-------|:--------|:------|:------------|
| 1992 - Dec 2006 | **Ralph Klein** | Progressive Conservative | Led Alberta through the oil boom; eliminated provincial debt by 2004-05; "Ralph Bucks" $400 rebate in 2005-06. Resigned Dec 2006. |
| Dec 2006 - Oct 2011 | **Ed Stelmach** | Progressive Conservative | Inherited the end of the oil boom; modified royalty framework; governed through the 2008-09 global financial crisis. Resigned Oct 2011. |
| Oct 2011 - Mar 2014 | **Alison Redford** | Progressive Conservative | Won 2012 election; governed through bitumen price discounts and declining resource revenue. Resigned Mar 2014 amid spending controversies. |
| Mar 2014 - Sep 2014 | **Dave Hancock** | Progressive Conservative | Interim Premier following Redford's resignation. |
| Sep 2014 - May 2015 | **Jim Prentice** | Progressive Conservative | Led through the beginning of the oil price collapse (WTI fell ~50% from Jun 2014 to Mar 2015). Lost the May 2015 election to the NDP. |
| May 2015 - Apr 2019 | **Rachel Notley** | NDP | First NDP government in Alberta history; governed through the worst of the oil price downturn; introduced carbon levy; championed Trans Mountain pipeline expansion. Lost Apr 2019 election. |
| Apr 2019 - Oct 2022 | **Jason Kenney** | United Conservative Party (UCP) | Formed the UCP by merging the PCs and Wildrose Party; governed through COVID-19 pandemic and the 2020 oil price crash; saw the return to surplus in 2021-22. Resigned Oct 2022 after a leadership review. |
| Oct 2022 - Present | **Danielle Smith** | United Conservative Party (UCP) | Won UCP leadership Oct 2022; won May 2023 general election; governing during a period of sustained surpluses driven by high oil and resource revenue. |

## Notes

1. **Reporting basis change at 2008-09**: Fiscal years 2000-01 through 2007-08 use the **Fiscal Plan basis** (excludes SUCH sector entities, pension liabilities, and Alberta Innovates corporations). Fiscal years 2008-09 onward use the **Consolidated Financial Statements basis** (includes all government entities). Dollar amounts are not strictly comparable across this boundary, but the surplus/deficit direction is consistent.

2. **Transition years**: Some fiscal years span a change in Premier (e.g., 2013-14, 2018-19, 2022-23). The budget for those years was typically tabled by the outgoing Premier, while the incoming Premier governed through the remainder.

3. **Resource revenue** includes all non-renewable resource royalties (bitumen, conventional oil, natural gas, land sales, etc.) and is the primary channel through which oil prices affect the provincial budget.

4. **All data sourced from** the 14 Budget and Annual Report PDFs in the `Budget PDFs/` folder, cross-referenced against the Historical Fiscal Summary tables included within those documents.
